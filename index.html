Research & Projects

Picard–Lindelöf theorem and maximal solutions
ODE reading project, UCL, 2024–25

A reading project on existence, uniqueness and maximal solutions for ODEs, done in conjunction with Aadam Imran, Wenqi Zhang, Runqian Jiang and Mafalda Mendes. Presented as a 15-minute group talk.

A favourite result

The Contraction Mapping theorem guarantees the existence of fixed points in a complete metric space $(X, d)$. If $f \colon X \to X$ is Lipschitz with constant $0 \leq K < 1$ then $\exists x^* \in X$ such that $f(x^*) = x^*$ and $x^*$ is unique.

As a corollary, this gives conditions on when elementary fixed point iteration methods can give roots of equations. Furthermore, it guarantees the existence and local uniqueness of solutions to ODEs via the Picard–Lindelöf theorem.
