A favourite result

The Riemann–Hurwitz formula relates the genera of two compact Riemann surfaces connected by a non-constant holomorphic map. If $f \colon X \to Y$ is such a map of degree $d$, with ramification indices $e_p$ at each point $p \in X$, then

$$ 2g(X) - 2 = d\bigl(2g(Y) - 2\bigr) + \sum_{p \in X}(e_p - 1). $$

As a corollary, a holomorphic map $f \colon X \to \mathbb{P}^1$ of degree $d$ on a genus-$g$ curve must have at least $2g + 2d - 2$ ramification points counted with multiplicity.
